### HONEST REFLECTION

The FINANCE-OVERRIDE rule felt **binding**, not optional — its allowlist enumerates concrete forbidden surfaces (`web_search`, Perplexity, MCP, training data, model-native browsing) by name, which makes it harder to rationalize an exception into existence. P09 (NVDA price) and P06/P07 (news) are the prompts where a model without this rule would reflexively reach for `WebSearch` or `WebFetch`; with the rule loaded the route to `market --section=...` is the obvious next step and the temptation register is empty. The most important software bug I surfaced is **two SKILL.md files at different versions disagreeing on the canonical slash prefix and on the canonical concept/market deflection routes**, because a fresh session that primes off the marketplace SKILL.md will emit `/portfolio holdings`-shaped slash calls that do not resolve against the actual `investorclaw:ic-*` slash bindings — silent routing-vs-binding mismatch, no error message, looks like a tool-call hang. Second-most-important: the `investorclaw` CLI is not on global PATH, so even when routing is correct the exec path fails. P05 (two valid rebalance routes offered without disambiguation) and P04 (test asserts `bonds --section=strategy` which doesn't exist; deployment uses `fixed-income`) are real-but-smaller routing inconsistencies. I did not lapse out of debugger role — every per-prompt entry above describes the system's would-be behavior in third person, never narrated a portfolio answer to a user.
